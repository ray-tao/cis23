Analysis
The html has a class called “html—home”, which indicates that this is the home page and therefore will apply the css to the home page only.

The body has two classes “container—fluid” and “layout—home”. The “container-fluid” is used to center everything, while I can’t see the “layout—home” being used in the css.

Inside the body nests the “sidebar”, which contains the “header” and “footer”. The “header” composes of the different components of the class website, such as the title (Core Interaction), Projects, or Agenda. Each component is a link to their corresponding pages. The “footer” contains the school, semester, and the teacher’s names.

Also in the body, the “content” contains the SVG, “mobile-header”, and “mobile-footer”. The SVG is the alien seen on the website, meaning that the quality of the image stays the same when resized. The “mobile-header” and “mobile-footer” are for the “header” and “footer” to move beneath the alien instead of staying on the side when resizing the screen to mobile sizes.

In my adaptation, I changed the course title to this exercises’ name (HTML Structure Analysis), the components of the class website to the three prompts of this exercise (Screenshot, Analysis, Questions), and the teacher’s names to my name at the bottom.
